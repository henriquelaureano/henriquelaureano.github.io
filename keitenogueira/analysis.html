CDI differences by Season

Season n CDI: Yes CDI: No pvalue
Summer 557 42 515 < 0.0001
Fall 494 39 455 < 0.0001
Winter 703 73 630 < 0.0001
Spring 518 43 475 < 0.0001

Basically, from the total n of each season, we want to know if the amount of people with CDI differs statistically from the amount without CDI.

The amounts are clearly different, whose differences are expressed in terms of highly significant p-values. In the table we simplified, the exact p-values are:

# A tibble: 4 × 2
  Season pvalue              
  <chr>  <chr>               
1 Summer 2.38706139426109e-89
2 Fall   3.61719616361755e-78
3 Winter 5.58267500222516e-98
4 Spring 2.44935652714071e-80

i.e., very smalls (e.g., 2.5e-5 = 0.000025).

To compute these p-values we used Pearson’s Chi-squared Tests (Agresti 2007; Patefield 1981).

Season effects

Besides looking marginally, at each season, we can look globally.

Analysis of Deviance Table (Type II tests)

Response: CDI
       LR Chisq Df Pr(>Chisq)
Season   3.8779  3      0.275

Also through a Pearson’s Chi-squared test, we see that when we compare all seasons there is no statistically significant difference between the prevalences.

Fitting a Bernoulli generalized linear model with logit link function, a.k.a. logistic regression (McCullagh and Nelder 1989; Venables and Ripley 2002), we are able to estimate the prevalences per season.

Season Summer 0.0754 0.0112 6.7395 < 0.0001 0.0535 0.0973

Season Winter 0.1038 0.0115 9.0251 < 0.0001 0.0813 0.1264
Season Spring 0.0830 0.0121 6.8475 < 0.0001 0.0593 0.1068

The highest prevalence is estimated in the winter, the smallest in the summer. The limits of 95% confidence interval are also provided. The p-values are based on the hypothesis: the estimated prevalence is significantly different from zero? They all are.

Also, we can test contrasts, i.e. differences between estimated effects. We test for all possibilities. The biggest difference, but still not statistically significant, is between the seasons of winter and summer. Basically, two-by-two, there is no statistical difference between the seasons.
